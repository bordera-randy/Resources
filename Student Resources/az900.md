## üéØ Exam Tips & Study Strategy

**Proven strategies to master AZ-900:**

1. **Complete Microsoft Learn modules first:**

   - Follow the official [Azure Fundamentals Learning Path](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/learn/paths/azure-fundamentals/)
   - Each module is short, interactive, and free
   - Takes about 3‚Äì4 hours to complete the entire path

2. **Watch one comprehensive video course:**

   - Reinforces concepts visually and contextually
   - Helps with long-term retention
   - Recommended: John Savill's AZ-900 Crash Course or ExamPro full course

3. **Understand the three domains proportionally:**

   - Cloud Concepts (25‚Äì30%): Foundation of all cloud knowledge
   - Azure Architecture & Services (35‚Äì40%): The bulk of the exam
   - Management & Governance (30‚Äì35%): Important for real-world scenarios

4. **Hands-on practice with Azure free tier:**

   - Create VMs, storage accounts, resource groups
   - Deploy a simple web app using App Service
   - Explore the Azure Portal and Azure CLI
   - No credit card required; $200 free credits

5. **Master key concepts before the exam:**

   - Cloud service models: IaaS, PaaS, SaaS (use cases)
   - Azure resource hierarchy: Subscriptions ‚Üí Resource Groups ‚Üí Resources
   - Shared responsibility model: Who does what (Microsoft vs Customer)
   - Azure services by category: Compute, Storage, Networking, Identity
   - Cost management: Pricing calculator, cost factors
   - Compliance and governance: Azure Policy, Microsoft Purview

6. **Review the Exam Skills Outline:**

   - Download the official PDF
   - Verify you understand every topic listed
   - Use as a final checklist before exam day

7. **Take practice tests multiple times:**

   - Use MeasureUp, Whizlabs, or Udemy practice exams
   - Aim for 80%+ before taking the real exam
   - Review every incorrect answer
   - Understand why the right answer is correct

8. **Study timeline recommendations:**
   - **Complete beginner:** 2‚Äì3 weeks (5‚Äì10 hours/week)
   - **Some IT background:** 1‚Äì2 weeks (5‚Äì10 hours/week)
   - **IT professionals:** 3‚Äì5 days (5‚Äì10 hours total)

**Exam day tips:**

- Arrive 15 minutes early for check-in
- Take a quick break halfway through if needed
- Read each question carefully; some have multiple correct aspects
- Don't overthink; trust your study and first instinct
- You only need 70% to pass (700 out of 1000 points)
